Congratulations! You Did It!

You’ve learned all thirty-three letters in the Russian alphabet. If you are excited about learning more Russian, we recommend that you go to the web resources to find out more.

There is, of course, no substitute for practice with a live teacher; you may wish to contact a local community college or adult education department to see if they have Russian classes.

In any case, we hope you have enjoyed this brief introduction to the Russian alphabet.

What would you like to do now?

  • Do a review exercise with all the letters.
  • Practice alphabetical order.
  • Learn about numbers in Russian.
  • See a page about Russian resources on the Internet.
  • See a page about Russian textbooks and dictionaries.
  • Go to Dave Eisenberg’s home page.
  • Send him some feedback.
